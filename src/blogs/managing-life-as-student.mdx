## Rise and Shine: The 5:30 AM Club

Okay, I can almost hear you groaning. "5:30 AM? Is this guy for real?" Yep, I'm 100% serious, and before you click away, hear me out!

My day kicks off at 5:30 AM, a good hour before the sun decides to make an appearance. Now, I'll be honest – I'm not one of those people who leap out of bed with a smile (if you are, what's your secret? comment it out!). But I've learned to embrace these early hours, and they've become my secret weapon.

From 5:30 to 7:00 AM, I dedicate time to my fitness routine. Some days, it's just refreshing to run a few kilometres in the nature, and I've this awesome perk that the Aravali mountains are real close to my place, so they are my go to spot in the morning. There's something magical about watching the world slowly wake up – the sound of temple bells in the distance, and the friendly nods from other early risers.

Other days, it's an invigorating workout session in my room. Push-ups, squats, and some more stuff to keep things interesting. Not only does this keep me physically fit, but it also sharpens my mind for the day ahead. Plus, those post-workout endorphins? Better than any cup of chai, I promise!

## The Secret Sauce: Finding Balance in Structure

Now, you might be wondering, "Harshit, how do you manage such a packed schedule without burning out?" The secret sauce is structure, my friends!

Each part of my day has a specific purpose, which helps me stay focused and efficient. But here's the kicker – the rest periods between activities are just as important as the activities themselves. They prevent burnout and help me switch gears between different modes of thinking.

For example, that power nap after lunch? It's non-negotiable. Those 20 minutes help me recharge and tackle my afternoon studies with renewed energy. And the family time during dinner prep? It's my way of staying connected with my loved ones and taking a mental break from academics.

<Callout type="warning">
  A word of caution: Maintaining such a structured schedule requires discipline and might not suit everyone. It's crucial to listen to your body and adjust your routine accordingly. Remember, the goal is progress, not perfection!
</Callout>

## Weekends: Time to Recharge and Explore

While my weekdays are pretty structured, I keep my weekends more flexible. This freedom allows me to:

1. **Dive deeper into coding projects**: Currently, I'm building a web app to help students to learn together and better with partners in their area. It's challenging, but so exciting!
2. **Extend my workout sessions**: Saturdays are for trying new fitness routines. Last week, I attempted rock climbing – let's just say I have a newfound respect for gravity.
3. **Catch up on schoolwork**: Because let's face it, sometimes we fall behind, and that's okay.
4. **Explore new interests**: I recently started learning Rust. The syntax was hard to catch up initially but now I just bear with it, it's hella fast tho
5. **Quality time with family and friends**: Because all work and no play makes Harshit a dull boy!

## Balancing Academics and Passions: My Not-So-Secret Formula

Now, you might be wondering, "How do you manage to keep up with your studies while dedicating time to fitness and coding?" It's all about efficiency and focus, my friends.

During school hours and my evening study time, I'm 100% focused on academics. No phone, no social media – just me and my books. The physical activity in the morning helps me stay alert during classes, and knowing I have my coding time to look forward to in the evening motivates me to finish my schoolwork efficiently.

As for coding, it's not just a hobby – it's an investment in my future. The logical thinking and problem-solving skills I develop while coding actually help me in subjects like Math and Physics. It's a win-win!

## Tips for Fellow Students: You've Got This!

If you're inspired to create a more balanced lifestyle, here are some tips to get you started:

1. **Start Small**: Don't try to overhaul your entire schedule at once. Begin with one small change and build from there.
2. **Prioritize Sleep**: Despite my late-night coding sessions, I ensure I get at least 7 hours of sleep. Good sleep is crucial for learning and overall well-being.
3. **Stay Flexible**: Some days won't go as planned, and that's okay. Be kind to yourself and adjust as needed.
4. **Find Your Peak Hours**: Schedule your most demanding tasks during the times when you're naturally most alert and productive.
5. **Make Time for Passions**: Don't sacrifice your interests for academics alone. Your hobbies and passions are what make you unique!
